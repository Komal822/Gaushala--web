Governing Document

Memorandum of Association

The Memorandum of Association (MoA) is the charter of our trust. It defines our name, registered office, aims & objectives, and the area of operation. It was filed with the Charity Commissioner at the time of registration.

Krushnai Goshala Trust – MoA

Main Objects of the Trust

  • To establish and maintain Gaushalas (cow shelters) for the protection, care, and welfare of cows, especially stray, injured, and abandoned cattle.
  • To provide food, fodder, water, and medical treatment to cows and other milch animals.
  • To promote the concept of cow protection and the importance of cows in Indian culture and agriculture.
  • To undertake rescue operations for cows in distress due to accidents, neglect, or natural calamities.
  • To work for the improvement of cattle breeds and promote organic farming using cow-based products.
  • To organise awareness camps, seminars, and training programs related to cow welfare and sustainable agriculture.
  • To cooperate with government and non-government agencies in achieving the above objectives.

Why the MoA matters

The Memorandum of Association is the foundational document of our trust. It sets out the legal framework within which we operate and assures donors, government bodies, and the public of our commitment to the stated charitable objectives.

  • Legal identity – It establishes the trust as a legal entity.
  • Scope of work – It defines what activities we can undertake.
  • Donor confidence – It assures that donations are used for the declared purposes.
