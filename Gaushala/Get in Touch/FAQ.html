All Questions

How can I donate to Krushnai Goshala Trust?

You can donate through multiple methods:

  • Online: Via our website using credit/debit card, UPI, or net banking
  • Bank Transfer: Direct transfer to our bank account (details available on request)
  • Cheque/DD: In favor of "Krushnai Goshala Trust"
  • Cash: At our Gaushala or local collection centers

All donations are eligible for 80G tax exemption.

Is my donation tax exempted under 80G?

Yes, Krushnai Goshala Trust is registered under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act. Donors are eligible for 50% tax exemption on their contributions. We provide a 80G receipt for all donations above ₹500.

Can I make a monthly recurring donation?

Absolutely! You can set up a monthly donation through our website. Choose your preferred amount and frequency (monthly/quarterly/yearly). Monthly donations help us plan our expenses better and ensure consistent care for our cows.

What is the minimum donation amount?

There is no minimum amount - every contribution, big or small, makes a difference! However, for 80G tax exemption certificate, donations above ₹500 are eligible. You can donate as little as ₹51 to feed a cow for a day.

Will I receive a receipt for my donation?

Yes, you will receive an automatic receipt via email for online donations. For offline donations, we will send a physical receipt within 7 working days. All receipts include 80G certification details.

How can I volunteer at the Gaushala?

You can volunteer by filling out our volunteer registration form online or visiting the Gaushala directly. Activities include feeding cows, cleaning shelters, assisting in medical camps, and helping with events. Minimum age requirement is 16 years (under 16 must be accompanied by an adult).

Do I need any special skills to volunteer?

No special skills are required - just a willingness to serve! However, if you have veterinary skills, fundraising experience, social media expertise, or teaching abilities, we'd love to utilize your talents. We provide all necessary training.

Can corporate groups volunteer together?

Yes! We welcome corporate groups for team-building and CSR volunteering. We can organize group activities, medical camps, cleanliness drives, and feeding programs. Please contact us in advance to schedule your visit.

What is the time commitment for volunteers?

We offer flexible options: one-time visits, weekend volunteering, or long-term commitments. Regular volunteers typically contribute 4-8 hours per week. You can choose what works best with your schedule.

How does cow adoption work?

Through our cow adoption program, you can sponsor the lifelong care of a cow. You pay an annual fee (₹11,001 per cow) that covers food, shelter, medical care, and maintenance. You'll receive a certificate, photo, and updates about your adopted cow. The cow remains at our Gaushala under our care.

Can I visit my adopted cow?

Absolutely! Adopters are welcome to visit their adopted cow during Gaushala visiting hours. We recommend scheduling your visit in advance so we can arrange a special moment for you with your cow.

Is cow adoption tax deductible?

Yes, cow adoption fees are eligible for 80G tax exemption as they are considered donations to the trust. You will receive a receipt for your adoption payment.

What is 80G tax exemption?

Section 80G of the Income Tax Act allows donors to claim a deduction on donations made to registered charitable trusts. Krushnai Goshala Trust is 80G registered, so donors can claim 50% tax exemption on their donations. For example, if you donate ₹10,000, you can deduct ₹5,000 from your taxable income.

How do I claim tax exemption for my donation?

Keep the donation receipt provided by Krushnai Goshala Trust. While filing your income tax returns, enter the donation amount under Section 80G deductions. You may need to show the receipt if requested by tax authorities.

Do I need to provide PAN for 80G certificate?

Yes, for donations above ₹2,500, we require your PAN card details to issue a valid 80G certificate. For donations below ₹2,500, PAN is optional but recommended.

What are the visiting hours?

We are open for visitors from 10:00 AM to 6:00 PM, Monday through Saturday. Sunday visits are by prior appointment only. The best time to visit is early morning (10 AM - 12 PM) or late afternoon (4 PM - 6 PM) when cows are most active.

Is there an entry fee?

No, there is no entry fee. However, we welcome voluntary contributions that help us maintain the Gaushala. Group visits of 10+ people should be scheduled in advance.

Can I bring food to feed the cows?

Yes, you can bring fruits, vegetables, or green fodder. However, please avoid processed foods, bread, or anything that might harm the cows. Our staff can guide you on appropriate feeding. You can also purchase approved fodder from our Gaushala.

How many cows does the Gaushala currently shelter?

We currently shelter over 750 cows, including injured, abandoned, and elderly cows. Our capacity is expanding with new shelters being constructed.

What medical facilities are available?

We have an in-house medical unit with a full-time veterinarian. Facilities include treatment rooms, isolation wards for sick animals, regular health checkups, vaccination programs, and emergency care. We also conduct medical camps for local farmers' cattle.

Is Krushnai Goshala registered with the government?

Yes, we are registered under the Bombay Public Trust Act, 1950. We hold 12A, 80G, and CSR-1 registrations, making us eligible for tax-exempt donations and corporate CSR partnerships. All our documents are available for public verification.
